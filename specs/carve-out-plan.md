# Resolving the carve-out issue: a layered plan

Status: planning doc, 2026-05-29. Scope: what it would take, at the Lean
level, to replace the "outcome regions carved to the Born value" pattern in
LF4 (`SingletKahler.lean`, the §14.2 `SpectralCarving` chain) with a genuine
derivation, and where the honest stopping line sits.

## 1. What "carve-out" means precisely

The instance-level LF4 results land on a Born value because the ontic object
is *defined* to carry that value, then verified to return it:

- `LF4/SingletKahler.lean`: `kMuPsi_kRegion` makes the fibre arc
  `fibreArc (P_st a b s t)` have `vol = P_st` by construction; `bridge_op_p`
  is then proved (Busch-free, via `born_rank_one_direct`) because the LHS was
  carved to the RHS.
- `LF4/SpectralCarving.lean`: `spectralRegion (bornWeights hA ψ) i` is the arc
  of length exactly `‖⟨uᵢ, ψ⟩‖²`; `diracProd_spectralRegion` returns
  `ofReal (w i)` because `w i` is that length.

Two facts compound the problem at every concrete `SectorData`:

1. **Regions carved to the Born length** (above).
2. **`Φ := id`** in every instance (`LF4/Instance.lean`, `KahlerInstance.lean`,
   `Tests/Examples.lean`). No deterministic dynamics is ever exercised, so
   LF1's "deterministic typicality" reduces, at the instance level, to an LLN
   over i.i.d. draws with no ontic evolution.

**Is it circular?** Not logically: every theorem is a valid, machine-checked
statement, and `lake build` is honest. It is question-begging at the level of
*physical explanation* — the answer is inserted as an input (arc length / `Φ`
absent) and recovered as output. The genuine content that survives is narrow
and real: the partition is honestly disjoint (`spectralRegion_pairwise_disjoint`),
and two structurally distinct routes (ontic Lebesgue integral vs Hilbert
Parseval) compute the same number (`integral_spectralOntic_eq_inner_re`). That
is a consistency check across machinery, not a derivation of the Born rule.

**Crucial framing (not a hidden defect).** Sigma0 is explicit that this is a
*sanctioned placeholder*, not a concealed gap:

- §2.3 / §5.7 (Sigma0 lines 501-508, 1097-1113): the ontic weight is
  `pᵢ = μL(Σᵢ)/μL(Ω₀)`; "This expression is **not yet the Born rule**. It is
  the ontic volume-ratio layer. The Born-form expression arises **only after**
  projection to epistemic space and the operational-consistency results
  developed elsewhere."
- §5.4 (lines 1034-1054): outcome regions are **posited** as a "context-defined
  partition," determined by a measurement context `M`.
- §9.5 (lines 1926-1947): "**The fourth open target** is the classification of
  physically realised measurement partitions. ... not every mathematical
  partition is physically admissible. A future theory should classify which
  partitions can be dynamically generated by realistic system-apparatus
  Hamiltonians. The target is a result of the form
  `HSA, pointer stability, null-boundary conditions ⟹ {Σᵢ(M)}`."

So the corpus does **not** claim to derive the outcome regions from dynamics;
it posits them and derives Born *form* (not the weights from geometry) via
symmetry + operational consistency. The Lean carving therefore does not
overclaim relative to the corpus. Resolving the carve-out = making progress on
the corpus's own open targets (G3b / §9.5), with TN3 as the constructive seed.

| Layer | Content | Sigma0 | Lean status |

The carve-out lives at **L2 + L3**. L1 is closed only modulo the Busch axiom
(itself an *encoding* of the Born step, not a derivation). L0 is partial.

## 3. Criterion for a genuine resolution

A non-circular result must define the outcome region `Σᵢ` by a construction
that **does not reference the Born value** — a flow basin, a metric/Voronoi
cell on `CP^{N-1}`, or a symmetry fundamental domain — and then prove

```
μL(Σᵢ) / μL(Ω₀) = (the Born value)
```

with the RHS *computed from* the construction. The region's definition must be
independent of the target number. Anything that sets `vol(Σᵢ) := target` and
verifies `vol(Σᵢ) = target` is carving, regardless of how much machinery wraps
the verification.

  `law(kFlow sh ∘ sampleₙ) = kMuL p₀`, hence the limit to the volume ratio).

This makes the LF1 deterministic-typicality theorem non-vacuous on the concrete

## 5. Recommended ordering and the honest stopping line

1. **Tranche A** first: cheap, removes the most embarrassing vacuity (`Φ = id`),
   and makes the LF1 deterministic-typicality claim non-trivial on the concrete
   instance. Low Mathlib risk.
2. **B0** (read TN3 + Papers C/D) before committing to B. The corpus may not yet
   derive basin volumes, in which case the achievable B-deliverable is
   "dynamically-defined partition + Born weight surfaced as an explicit
   hypothesis," not a full derivation. Decide scope from what TN3 actually
   proves.
3. **Tranche B** proper if and only if TN3 supplies an independent basin-volume
   computation; otherwise the honesty-improvement form of B.
4. **Tranche C** is long-term and parallel.

**Honest stopping line (matches Sigma0 §9.5).** No physical theory derives its
own measurement partition for free; the legitimate target is
`HSA + pointer-stability ⟹ partition`, which the corpus lists as open. The
single deliverable that would let us drop a "constructed-to-fit" caveat is
Tranche B's independent basin-volume theorem. Absent that, Tranche A plus
surfacing the Born-weight import as an explicit hypothesis (B-honesty form) is
the maximum we can claim without overstating. The carving is not a bug to hide;
it is the Lean shadow of debt G3b, and resolving it *is* doing the open physics.
