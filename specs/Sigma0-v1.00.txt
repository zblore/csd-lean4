                                                ABSTRACT

0 states the ontic commitments underlying Constraint-Surface Dynamics. The paper treats physical

reality as deterministic Hamiltonian evolution on a compact finite-dimensional Kähler manifold

, equipped with Liouville measure µL, while quantum state space CPN-1 is interpreted as an

epistemic       projection  through  a  smooth  many-to-one  map    :           N  -1  .  Its  purpose  is  not  to

derive quantum mechanics, spacetime, or relativistic structure from first principles, but to make

explicit the assumptions required by the existing finite-dimensional CSD programme.

The paper defines the roles of , , µL, -time, -energy, measurement, and contextual nonlocality.
Measurement is interpreted as a -level de-isolation event, while spacetime measurement records
are treated as coarse-grained images of deeper ontic dynamics. Bell-type correlations are therefore
accepted as nonlocal relative to emergent spacetime, while leaving open whether they are structurally
local in .

The contribution of 0 is deliberately limited. It does not derive , , spacetime, c, Planck-
scale resolution, cosmological-scale bounds, or the mechanism connecting -local structure to
spacetime-distant correlations. Instead, it provides an internal ontological foundation for later work
on approximate projectability, emergent spacetime, causal order, and effective locality.

1 Introduction

1.1 Purpose of the Paper

This paper states the ontic commitments underlying Constraint-Surface Dynamics. Its purpose is not to derive the full
quantum formalism, spacetime, or relativistic structure from first principles. Its purpose is narrower: to make explicit
the physical picture assumed by the existing finite-dimensional CSD programme.

The central posit is that the fundamental physical state is an ontic microstate

                                x  ,

where  is a compact finite-dimensional Kähler manifold equipped with Liouville measure µL and deterministic
Hamiltonian flow. The quantum state is not treated as the fundamental physical state. Rather, the projective state

is interpreted as an epistemic image of deeper ontic structure under a smooth many-to-one projection

This distinction is the organising principle of the paper. The ontic level contains the actual state and deterministic
dynamics. The epistemic level contains the projective quantum description. The operational level contains laboratory
probabilities, measurement records, and effective quantum predictions.

1.2 Why 0 Is Needed

The existing CSD corpus already contains several technical layers: deterministic volume typicality, the finite-dimensional
measure bridge, Born-form operational weights, mixed states, POVMs, sequential update, Bell-sector constructions, and
formal Lean milestones. These results rely on a deeper ontological picture, but that picture should not remain implicit.

Without an explicit ontic foundation, later papers risk two errors. First, they may appear to derive structures that are
currently assumed. Second, they may blur the distinction between quantum-effective results and deeper claims about
spacetime, locality, and causation.

0 is therefore an internal clarification document. It separates:

1.3 Core Ontological Commitments

The paper adopts the following commitments.
First, physical reality is represented by deterministic dynamics on . A physical system has an actual ontic state x, and
its evolution is generated by Hamiltonian flow on the ontic manifold.
Second, the Liouville measure µL is the relevant invariant volume measure on . It supplies the measure-theoretic
backbone for volume typicality and frequency convergence.
Third, quantum state space is an epistemic projection of the ontic structure. The map

                                                             :   CPN-1

identifies ontic states that are operationally indistinguishable at the projective quantum level.
Fourth, measurement is interpreted as a -level de-isolation event. A subsystem that was approximately projectable
ceases to evolve autonomously when it becomes dynamically coupled to apparatus or environmental degrees of freedom.
Fifth, CSD is contextual and nonlocal relative to emergent spacetime. It does not assume Bell-local outcome functions.
It instead treats outcomes as context-indexed consequences of the realised system-apparatus interaction.
Sixth, spacetime is treated as emergent rather than primitive. The detailed map from -level structure to spacetime
structure is not specified in this paper.

1.4 Main Non-Claims

The status of this paper is deliberately conservative. 0 does not claim to complete the CSD programme.
It does not derive the existence of . It does not derive the Kähler structure of . It does not derive the projection . It
does not derive spacetime, Lorentzian metric structure, or relativistic causal cones. It does not prove that the -level
causal rate is identical to the observed speed of light. It does not derive Planck-scale or cosmological-scale resolution
bounds. It does not provide a complete -level mechanism for Bell correlations.
These are not treated as hidden results. They are explicitly identified as research targets.
The paper should therefore be read as an ontological foundation and assumptions audit, not as a final first-principles
derivation.

1.5 Position Within the CSD Programme

0 sits below the existing technical reconstruction papers. Paper A supplies the deterministic repeated-trial and
