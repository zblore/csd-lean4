                                           ABSTRACT

This paper reconstructs the operational content of finite-dimensional quantum mechanics from
deterministic Hamiltonian dynamics defined on a compact Kõhler manifold . An ontic microstate
evolves under a Liouville-preserving flow generated by a Hamiltonian vector field, while observable
statistics arise only after applying a smooth many-to-one projection  :   CP n-1 to epistemic
coordinates. The induced epistemic measure is taken to be absolutely continuous with respect to the
Fubini¡Study measure, which is selected as the canonical invariant density under the symmetry and
regularity assumptions developed in companion work.
For the quantum-effective sector defined by Axiom A5, the projected dynamics on CPn-1 defines
a holomorphic vector field. By the Ashtekar¡Schilling correspondence, this yields the standard
Schr÷dinger form of unitary evolution on the operational state space. Measurement interactions are
modelled as Hamiltonian evolution on a joint system¡apparatus surface, with outcomes represented by
context-dependent measurable regions on CPn-1. Outcome probabilities are assigned by integrating
the prepared epistemic density over these regions; in the finite-dimensional setting, the admissible
Born-form weight assignment is fixed in companion work, while the mixed-state, POVM, and
reduced-state representation layers are completed in TN4. In the projective examples studied here,
the outcome regions admit explicit Fubini¡Study Voronoi realisations.

Accordingly, the present paper should be read as the finite-dimensional reconstruction architecture
for CSD: it specifies the ontic projection framework, the quantum-effective dynamical sector, and
the context-to-region measurement map, while relying on companion results for the Born-weight
theorem and the full operational dictionary.

1 Introduction

1.1 Background

Standard finite-dimensional quantum mechanics is formulated on a Hilbert space H = Cn, with pure states represented
by rays []  CP n-1 and probabilities given by the Born rule

                                                              P (i) = |i|2.

Although operationally successful, this axiomatic structure does not explain why the Hilbert space geometry, the
Fubini¡Study measure ÁFS, or the Born weights arise from underlying physical principles. Measurement is introduced
as an external postulate, and no mechanism is provided for the emergence of tensor-product structure, decoherence, or
contextuality. This motivates a reconstruction of quantum mechanics based on geometric and dynamical principles
rather than algebraic axioms.

The conventional formalism treats the state vector  as fundamental, with its linear structure and probabilistic interpre-
tation assumed rather than derived. The Born rule is postulated independently of any ontic structure. Measurement
outcome geometry is not connected to physical interactions, and the composite-system tensor product has no geometric
justification. These limitations motivate an ontic framework in which statistical structure emerges from dynamics on an
underlying symplectic manifold (, ).

The approach developed in this paper should be understood as a reconstruction rather than a derivation. We begin
with a compact Kõhler manifold , a projection map  :   CPn-1, and the Fubini¡Study geometry of projective

1.2 Aim of This Paper

The objective of this work is to show that the full operational formalism of finite-dimensional quantum mechanics arises
from deterministic Hamiltonian dynamics on  together with the geometric structure of CP n-1.

The reconstruction developed here differs in important respects from standard interpretations of quantum mechanics.
Unlike Bohmian mechanics, the ontic dynamics occur on a compact Kõhler manifold  rather than in configuration
space, and no guiding equation is introduced. Unlike Everettian interpretations, no branching ontology is assumed;
instead, the epistemic state reflects coarse-grained information under the projection . No stochastic collapse primitives
are added, distinguishing the framework from GRW-type theories. While the formalism shares geometric structure
with Geometric Quantum Mechanics, it supplements this with a lower-level deterministic dynamics whose projection

1.3 Problems Addressed

The reconstruction developed in this paper addresses three interconnected levels of structure: the ontic geometry on
, the epistemic dynamics on CP n-1, and the operational predictions accessible to observers. Each level presents
specific problems that must be resolved in order to derive the finite-dimensional quantum formalism from deterministic
Hamiltonian dynamics.

structures required for quantum phenomena. We assume that  is a finite-dimensional compact Kõhler manifold
equipped with symplectic form , complex structure J, and compatible metric g. The Hamiltonian vector field XH
generated by a real-valued Hamiltonian H must preserve the Liouville measure ÁL associated with . A central problem
at the ontic level is to identify the conditions under which the pushforward of the ontic vector field,

is linear in the epistemic coordinates  and therefore capable of generating standard quantum dynamics. For Hamiltoni-
ans in the quantum-effective sector, the resulting projected flow is Hamiltonian up to controlled corrections of order
O(), and in the ideal limit  = 0 it becomes holomorphic and admits a linear representation on Cn.

1.4 Scope and Limitations

The reconstruction developed in this paper applies only to finite-dimensional, nonrelativistic quantum systems. All
results are formulated on the epistemic manifold CP n-1 and rely on the compactness and finite-dimensional Kõhler
geometry of the ontic surface . The analysis does not apply to infinite-dimensional Hilbert spaces, continuous spectra,
quantum fields, or continuous-mode systems, which require noncompact projective spaces and infinite-dimensional
symplectic geometry.

The present work does not treat identical-particle sectors. Bosonic and fermionic symmetries require additional
geometric structure that is developed separately in Paper , where permutation symmetry and indistinguishability are
incorporated into both the ontic and epistemic manifolds. The emergence of relativistic spacetime geometry is also
not addressed. No relativistic structure is introduced in this paper, and the derivation of Lorentzian geometry, causal
ordering, and relativistic dynamics is carried out in Paper .

The reconstruction presented here assumes the following structural elements. First, there exists a compact Kõhler
manifold (, , J, g) carrying a Liouville-preserving Hamiltonian flow XH , together with a smooth many-to-one
projection  :   CP n-1 whose pushforward ÁL is absolutely continuous with respect to the Fubini¡Study
measure ÁFS and compatible with SU(n) symmetry. Second, at the epistemic level the analysis assumes only the
results of Papers A and B: that long-run frequencies follow invariant volumes on  and that ÁFS is the unique
symmetry-compatible measure on CP n-1.

Given these assumptions, the remainder of the paper establishes the finite-dimensional reconstruction architecture used
in CSD. More precisely, it shows how a closed Schr÷dinger-form epistemic dynamics arises in the quantum-effective

dependence is encoded geometrically. The present paper does not by itself prove every element of the finite-dimensional

subsystem-reduction layers are completed in TN4. Likewise, the existence and regularity of  and  are assumed rather
than derived, and the deeper physical origin of the quantum-effective sector selected by A5 is left for later work.

3.3 Non-linear flows and the quantum-effective sector

The deterministic Hamiltonian flows that exist on the ontic manifold  are generally more numerous than those that

4.2 Non-factorisation of the ontic space

6.2 Contextuality from Context-Dependent Partitions

In this framework contextuality arises from the incompatibility of context-dependent measurement partitions on the
epistemic space CP n-1. Each measurement context M generates a distinct family of outcome regions {i(M )}, and
different contexts generally induce different partitions because the interaction Hamiltonian selects different stability
directions of the projected flow (see Section 5.5). In the projective examples studied here, these partitions are realised
concretely by Fubini¡Study Voronoi constructions. The resulting partitions are generally incompatible, meaning that
their boundaries do not align and there is no common refinement that represents both simultaneously.

The deterministic microstate    does not assign values to all observables at once. The outcome revealed by
a measurement depends on which region i(M ) the epistemic state [] = () occupies after the system has
been coupled to the apparatus implementing context M . Changing the context changes the interaction Hamiltonian
and therefore changes the outcome partition that defines outcomes. Determinism at the ontic level does not imply
value-definiteness across incompatible contexts.
Because probabilities are computed from a prepared epistemic state ep or equivalently from ^, changing context
changes the measurable event i(M ) and the associated projector family {i(M )}, not a context-independent raw
volume assigned to an outcome label.
This resolves the Kochen¡Specker obstruction. No global value map on CP n-1 is introduced, and no assumption of
noncontextuality is made. The model is therefore consistent with the Kochen¡Specker theorem and with the constraints
of Bell and Fine. Contextual behaviour emerges from the fact that each measurement context induces its own coarse-
graining of the epistemic manifold through its associated outcome partition, while the deterministic flow on  remains
well defined throughout. In the projective examples studied here, these partitions admit explicit Fubini¡Study Voronoi
realisations.
Contextuality in this reconstruction is a geometric property of how different measurement interactions partition CP n-1
and not a sign of any inconsistency in the underlying ontic dynamics.
